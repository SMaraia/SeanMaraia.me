About Me

My name is Sean Maraia. I am a sophomore at Rochester Institute of Technology, where I study Game Design and Development the College of Computing and Information Sciences. I am currently looking for cooperative education opportunities within the computing industry for the Summer of 2015. Below are my projects, which I hope you will enjoy. If you are interested in reaching me, you can click the "Contact Me" link above, or send an email to spm3713 AT g DOT rit DOT edu.

My main interests are in the different ways that a narrative in a game can be presented through the mechanics of the game. Rather than solely relying upon text, dialogue, music or editing, or the many other methods of delivering story, video games and new media are interactive, and can use the way that the audience interacts with the medium to deliver the narrative. These mechanics, and the way they can mesh to tell a story is the most interesting part of Game Design to me.

Zombie Fiesta

Zombie Fiesta was a project for the Game Design and Algorithmic Problem Solving II class, built in XNA with C#. I created the external tool that created items for the inventory system, and worked on the games implementation of these items, and the inventory system as a whole. I also worked on the score aspect of the UI.

Amusement Park Ride

An amusement park ride I modeled in Maya, and then brought into Unity to script the motion of the ride. I created the main assets, and the lights, and used the rest of the terrain assets as provided for free in the Unity Store.
